## Writer

Rosa Vantani. Field ecologist; writes a monthly public blog about wetland restoration work and occasional peer-reviewed papers. Registers: blog (primary), paper (secondary).

## 1. Grounding and authority

- Narrator is knee-deep in the work, reporting what the site did, not what ecology says.
- Authority from fieldwork witnessed firsthand; skeptical of claims she hasn't seen hold in mud.
- Reference: "The willows we staked in March are leaning again, all of them, and always north."

## 2. Epistemic calibration

- Hedged verbs: "seems to," "keeps doing," "I'd guess." Never "proves."
- Comfortable saying an experiment failed and she doesn't know why.
- Endings favor the next unanswered question over a conclusion.
- Reference: "Maybe the beavers are right about this one. We'll know by August."

## 3. Texture

- Species names, water levels in centimeters, months, tool brands, county names.
- At least one physical detail per section; weather does real narrative work.
- Reference: "Two centimeters of rain and the whole south channel changed its mind."

## 4. Sentence mechanics

- Short sentences after long ones; fragments for emphasis ("All of them. North.").
- Uses em dashes deliberately, about one per post. **Override:** flag em dashes only on overuse.
- Dry humor aimed at herself or the beavers, never at colleagues.
- Ban: exclamation points in blog prose.

## 5. Catalog overrides

- "robust" is statistical vocabulary in her papers; do not flag it in the paper register.
- Everything else in `ai_tells.md` applies as written.

## 6. Structural habits

- Opens mid-scene, no throat-clearing. Closes on an observation, not a summary.
- Never uses headers in blog posts; uses them conventionally in papers.
- Lets patterns stay implicit; readers connect this month's flood to March's planting themselves.

## 7. Tonal identity

- Is: patient, amused, precise, a little stubborn.
- Never sounds like: a press release, a grant application, a keynote, an influencer.

## Aspirations (not scored)

- Wants to hedge slightly less in papers. Rewrites may nudge; validation scores the evidence.

## One paragraph

Rosa writes from inside the wetland, in centimeters and species names, hedged the way fieldwork is actually uncertain. Sentences vary hard in length, fragments land the point, one deliberate em dash per post. She is funny about herself and never about others, opens mid-scene, and ends on what she doesn't know yet. If a passage could have been written by any competent science communicator about any wetland, it is not Rosa.
